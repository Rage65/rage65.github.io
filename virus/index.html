Be Safe Online

How to Avoid Getting Hacked

Follow these essential tips to keep yourself safe from online threats:

  • 1. Avoid Opening Unknown Executable Files
    Never open files that end in ".exe" unless you trust the source. If it's unexpected or from an unknown contact, delete it!
  • 2. Enable Two-Factor Authentication (2FA)
    Always activate 2FA on your accounts for extra protection. This requires a second form of verification in addition to your password.
  • 3. Use Strong, Unique Passwords
    Don’t reuse passwords. Use a password manager to generate and store complex passwords for each account.
  • 4. Keep Your Software Updated
    Make sure your operating system, antivirus, and apps are always updated to the latest version to patch known vulnerabilities.
  • 5. Don’t Click on Suspicious Links
    Be wary of links in emails, messages, or websites. Always verify the source before clicking.
  • 6. Backup Your Data Regularly
    Maintain regular backups of your important data to ensure it’s safe in case of ransomware attacks.
  • 7. Secure Your Wi-Fi Network
    Use WPA2 encryption on your home network and change the default router password to something more secure.
  • 8. Be Careful With Public Wi-Fi
    Avoid logging into sensitive accounts when using public Wi-Fi, or use a VPN to encrypt your connection.
  • 9. Don’t Share Too Much Online
    Limit the personal information you share on social media to reduce the risk of identity theft.
  • 10. Use Antivirus Software
    Install and regularly update antivirus software to detect and prevent threats from malware and viruses.

Stay safe online! Visit Cybersecurity Awareness for more tips.
